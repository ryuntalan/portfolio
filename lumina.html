Lumina
Ryan Untalan
Dec. 12, 2020 · 12 min
Overview
Project Description: Lumina is a flexible digital space divider that creates a personal work environment that promotes mental well-being. Lumina was created during a graduate studio course at the University of Washington.
Role: Product Designer and UX Researcher
Tools: Figma, Principle, Miro, Spline
Time Duration: 10 weeks
Project Context
For the University of Washington's MHCI+D ideation studio course, our cohort was challenged to take a speculative approach to designing for the home and household. My team and I aligned on creating a product that would help professionals who primarily work from home.
Problem Space
Working from home is the new normal and it is here to stay.
In an international survey of 2285 remote workers, 71% of managers and employees desire work-from-home options even after the pandemic is over. However, working from home can intrude both physically and psychologically on family time, blurring the boundaries between home and work, and encourages overwork and work preoccupation. The lack of dedicated workspaces, competing priorities at home, and increase in distractions lead to more mental fatigue and poor mental well-being.
Design Challenge
How might we help professionals working from home improve their mental well-being by creating personalized ways to manage mental fatigue?
Design Response
Lumina creates a personalized work environment that promotes mental well-being
Customize Lumina to suit your mood with the companion mobile app
Results
Our team won first place at our cohort design showcase, and Lumina was awarded the most innovative project.

The Design Process
Design Space
Support a mentally healthy work environment for professionals working at home. We defined professionals as employees who have to acquire, create, and apply knowledge for the purpose of their work; not manual labor.
Stakeholder Mapping
We begin with creating a stakeholder map to provide us with a visual representation of the various groups involved within the design space. Stakeholder mapping helped us think critically about who does and doesn’t benefit within our space and helped us frame our research questions and design prompt.
Research Questions
To gain a deeper understanding of professionals working from home, we determined research questions to scope out our research plan. Our 3 core research questions are:
  1. What does a mentally healthy work environment look like?
  2. What are the barriers to a mentally healthy work environment at home?
  3. What supports a mentally healthy work environment at home?
Literature Review
Our key findings from our literature review:
  1. People’s physical environment can affect their well-being. Many physical factors within the work environment such as temperature, lighting, room color, noise level, and the amount of interior plants can all affect one’s mental well-being.
  2. Remote workers may overwork. Individuals who work remotely may frequently experience feelings of guilt and may overwork to reciprocate the perceived flexibility.
  3. Blurred home-work boundaries. Remote workers may engage in behaviours such as exchanging emails during non-working hours, a practice that has been linked to stress and blurred home-work boundaries.
  4. Boundaries give people time to recover. Setting work and home life boundaries gives people time to recover from the demands and pressures of work.
Contextual Inquiry
We conducted four contextual inquiries with professionals who primarily work from home. They invited us into their homes to give us a virtual tour of their workspace and home environment and speak about how it affects their mental well-being.
Insights
  1. Professionals feel mentally healthy when they can do their best at work, take care of themselves, and feel safe to express their opinions.
    • Mentally healthy means that you are in a good mindset to do whatever you need to do, to accomplish whatever tasks you have in a way that you don’t feel overwhelmed -P1
  2. Professionals who work from home experience mental fatigue and guilt due to increased self-accountability and responsibility.
    • All these hats are on my head. When you have multiple hats on your head at one time, it feels like a heavier mental load. -P3
  3. Working from home increases mental fatigue by making it difficult for professionals to separate their different life roles and responsibilities.
    • When you are working from home, it all bleeds into each other. So it’s hard to separate that mentally. -P4
  4. Professionals who have to share their workspace with other people experience more mental fatigue when they have to adapt to find privacy and avoid distractions.
    • When people come down the stairs, I'm hyper conscious of that... it's ever present in my mind - P1
Research Summary
In summary, professionals working at home:
  1. Feel mentally healthy when they can do their best at work and take care of themselves.
  2. Experience mental fatigue* due to a variety of factors that are unique to their work and personal life.
  3. Find ways to adapt to the challenges of working at home given their individual limitations of their household.
*Mental fatigue: caused by long-term cognitive activities, mental fatigue is mainly manifested as drowsiness, difficulty in concentrating, decreased alertness, disordered thinking, slow reaction, lethargy, reduced work efficiency, and error-prone.
Design Challenge
Informed by our insights, we created the following design challenge to help us define and focus in on the people and problems we’re designing for:
How might we help professionals working from home improve their mental well-being by creating personalized ways to manage mental fatigue given the limits of their home?
Desired Outcomes
We also defined desired outcomes to serve as a reminder throughout the design process that we’re working towards a design response that is based on the needs of professionals working from home.
  1. Improve the mental well-being of professionals working from home.
  2. Develop a personalized way to reduce mental fatigue so professionals feel more energized to do both work and personal activities.
  3. Easy integration into any home.
Brainstorm 90 Ideas
In response to the design challenge, each of us sketched 30 ideas. We then plotted our 90 ideas on a 2x2 matrix and affinitized. Major themes include: ways to divide workspaces in the home (physical/ digital), smart/ adaptable household items and furniture, wearables, digital or physical products for self-care and relaxation, and mixed reality/ VR environments.
Down-Selecting with Dot Voting
We then used a dot voting method to narrow down our 90 ideas. Each of us voted for 5 ideas that either stood out or sparked interest. 10 ideas remained.
Design Principles
Leveraging our desired outcomes and insights, we crafted 4 design principles to help us further down select. Design principles are important in creating a shared evaluation language and helped my team and I align on our product values.
Top 3 Ideas
After discussing each idea, we narrowed down to our top 3 ideas.
Storyboarding & Critiques
We refined and made a storyboard for each idea to illustrate the high-level experience. Then presented each concept to our peers and instructors. The critique session focused on two main things: which product addresses mental well-being the most and which product is the most exciting/ interesting.

Establishing a Design Direction
As we continue to refine our concept, it’s important to establish a design direction so that we're aligned on both the visual and emotional aspect of our product. I led my team through this process by first identifying our brand values. Through a brainstorming session, we generated words that represent our design principles. I then synthesized them into 4 brand values/ attributes: soothing, harmonious, personal, and approachable. We then dived into creating moodboards that represent those values.
FlexiScreen to Lumina
A modern look with soft and warm colors. We want to create a personal and soothing experience, with a clean and welcoming feel. Also, we decided to change the name of FlexiScreen to Lumina because Lumina felt more soothing and welcoming.
Refining the Concept
Lumina is a flexible digital space divider that creates a personal work environment that promotes mental well-being. By establishing both physical and mental separation in your workspace, Lumina gives you more privacy, less distractions, and better boundaries between your work and home.
Information Architecture
I made a task flow for setting up Lumina and created the mobile app information architecture. These diagrams illustrate key interaction points and allow us to get feedback and iterate on them before we invest time into prototyping.
Key Path Prototype Planning
With the first iteration of our core concept and information architecture set, we begin prototype planning. We defined 3 key paths accompanied by a set of research questions to test with our low-fidelity prototypes. Prototype planning helps with establishing an aligned agenda on what questions we want to answer and helps ensure we’re using our time wisely when creating low-fidelity prototypes. Here are our key paths:
Key Path 1: Physically setting up Lumina and experiencing a new work environment
Key Path 2: Using the mobile application to customize Lumina and set up a schedule
Key Path 3: Communicating availability status to others while using Lumina
Prototype Insights & Next Steps
Key Path 1 Insights
  1. More interactive features. Professionals are interested in adding more features to Lumina, like an interactive display, digital sticky notes, and more background display options.
  2. Concern about screen fatigue. There was concern about fatigue due to light and color from the display. Some thought that moving patterns may be too stimulating, and wanted options to display static pictures of scenic background images instead.
  3. Better separation. Most were enthusiastic about being able to create their own space/ bubble with better ambience, describing it as a fancy digital cubicle. One user wondered if it would be claustrophobic.
  4. Adaptable physical form. Professionals want to customize the physical form of Lumina to use it in a variety of locations in the home. For example, being able to stretch the product to full size or extended on the table.
Key Path 1 Next Steps
  1. Consider a modular design. We want to explore the possibility of building this divider with smaller modules, which will be connected by magnets. We will also consider other interactive features that can be enabled by this modular form.
  2. Explore interactive features for mental well-being. Many professionals are interested in interacting with the display, such as using it for meetings or as a digital whiteboard. While we can see the utility of these ideas, we are more interested in interactivity as a way to enhance mental well-being over productivity. We will explore using the display for interactive activities for mindfulness, meditation, or relaxation.
  3. Adjust how we present Lumina. We need to emphasize that Lumina is not a digital “screen” similar to a TV or monitor due to concerns about screen fatigue. Feedback revealed that this was a big concern for users and we will be sticking to the use of light and colors.
Key Path 2 Insights
  1. Convenient way to customize. Professionals thought the mobile application was a convenient way to adjust the settings without having to get up. They also wanted to save their customized settings to use for next time.
  2. Scheduled breaks have value. Professionals see the value in scheduling the display to change at certain times, both as a reminder to take breaks and for other reasons, such as notifying them of calendar events.
  3. Prompting breaks. Professionals did not think a change in color in the display would be be enough to stop them while they were working on something important. However, it could still serve as a reminder as they would notice it and take a break when they were finished their task.
Key Path 2 Next Steps
  1. Improve the onboarding experience. To improve our onboarding experience, we plan on adding a section that introduces the key features of the Lumina mobile app: the control panel and scheduling.
  2. Refine the physical and mobile UI. We want to continue to polish and refine the physical and mobile user interface. We plan on establishing a design system to help create a cohesive and consistent design.
  3. Add a save custom settings feature. Adding a save custom settings feature will allow professionals to create and save their own custom color and brightness settings. This will help create a more personalized experience and streamline the process for finding the ideal settings.
Key Path 3 Insights
  1. A need to communicate. Professionals who shared their space with others liked the idea of communicating their work status to others using Lumina.
  2. Communicate nuanced situations. Some professionals wished to communicate customized statuses on Lumina’s exterior to provide more information than the three options we provided. Others felt the options were sufficient.
  3. Colors influence emotions. Colors on Lumina’s exterior can affect the mood of others in the space and should be carefully considered.
  4. Subtle signifiers. User did not think it was necessary to use the entire exterior of Lumina to communicate their status (e.g. big wall of color). They suggested using smaller signifiers instead, e.g. message banner or lights at the top of the screen.
Key Path 3 Next Steps
  1. Keep the exterior simple. We want to ensure that Lumina is non-intrusive within the household. Colors can affect one’s mood and we want to prevent Lumina from influencing the moods of non-users within the household. Keeping the exterior simple means that we will remove the color feature and go with just text.
  2. Implement customizable texts. Customizable texts will allow flexible communication. Instead of limiting to 3 statuses (do not disturb, working, and available) users will be able to display their own personal message on the exterior to help communicate nuanced situations.
Design System
I created the Lumina mobile app design system within Figma. This helped ensure we have a cohesive visual design throughout the interface. The visual decisions were guided by our established design direction. We went with a light lavender purple as our primary color to create a soothing feel. We used Proxima Nova as our primary typeface because of its clean, modern, and friendly look.
Key Features + Final Designs
Create a private workspace
Lumina creates both physical and mental separation in your workspace, giving you more privacy, less distractions, and better boundaries between your work and home.
Personalize your Lumina
Use the mobile app or the interactive menu on Lumina to customize and save color, brightness, and opacity preferences to suit your mood.
Schedule breaks
You can schedule Lumina to change at scheduled times to remind you to take breaks throughout the day.
Communicate availability
Use Lumina to display a custom message to others in your home about your availability, minimizing mental fatigue from interruptions.
Storable
Lumina can be rolled up and stored away when you are done for the day, allowing your space to take on multiple purposes for work and life.
Specification Document
To wrap up this project, my team and I created a specification document which includes Lumina conceptual model details, information architecture, interaction flows, our design system, and redlining. We created this document to communicate specific design details to ensure that our Lumina concept would be implemented in the way we intended.
Reflection
Ask why often. When designing a product, it’s important to understand the purpose behind a selected design method or design decision. Asking why often ensures that we’re checking the reasoning behind a decision and helps prevent biased/ uninformed/ blind decisions. In addition, by knowing and communicating our reasoning, we can get better feedback on decisions and it can check for assumptions.
Team alignment is an evolving process. Whether it’s through defining a design space, brand values, or core features, it’s important to encourage the sharing of ideas and engage in dialogue with each other to find alignment in order to move forward.
